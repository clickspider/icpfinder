**Body:**

```
My last project was a marketplace called InsiderMatch. I sent 8 personalized Loom videos to hand-picked prospects. Zero paying customers. Three weeks of follow-up, polite passes and ghosts.

8 is too small a sample to call statistical anything. Top-quartile Loom outreach hits 22-30% reply (builtforb2b 2025 benchmark). Cold email floor is 1-5%. So 0/8 could be variance. I sat in that ambiguity for two weeks before admitting the real answer. I had picked the wrong 8 people. I had no ICP. I had a confident-sounding guess.

Paul Graham, How to Make Wealth: "When you're starting a business, it's easy to slide into thinking that customers want what you do." That was me.

CB Insights puts "no market need" at the top of the failed-startup list, 35-42%. The indie canon says "talk to customers" but quietly assumes you know which ones. For a solo builder shipping AI products in a week, the upstream blocker isn't execution. It's ICP definition. And the tools that solve ICP are priced for funded teams. Apollo runs $50-119/seat/mo plus credit add-ons that hit $600-1,000/mo. Clearbit and HubSpot Breeze sit at $1K-$5K/mo. ZoomInfo's median verified contract is $31,875/yr per Vendr.

So I spent 5 days building the tool I needed.

## icpfinder.dev

Paste a product description. Get 3 buyer archetypes, each with a buying thesis (pain, trigger, budget, objections), plus verified contact emails per archetype.

pain: "differentiating from Google Analytics on trust + speed"
trigger: "GDPR/cookie-consent fatigue in the industry"
budget: "$20-100/mo, no procurement"
email: marko@plausible.io (score 99)
\`\`\`

## The model

MIT licensed. BYOK is the headline, not the footnote. Bring a Gemini key and a Hunter key. Both live in browser localStorage. They never hit my server. Hosted demo is free, capped at 1 run/day so I don't get a surprise bill. $20/mo removes the cap.

Gemini 2.5 Flash bills $2.50 per million output tokens. Each run costs you fractions of a cent. Hunter's free tier (50 lookups/mo) covers a tire-kicker.

## What it won't do

Hunter accuracy drops to ~43% on early-stage targets vs ~78% on Fortune 500. 23% of returned emails belong to former employees. LLM drift on niche verticals can hit 50%. The email layer is the weakest link, not the LLM. Treat output as a research starting point, not a CRM import.

Repo and hosted demo: github.com/clickspider/icpfinder, icpfinder.dev. Run it on your product and post the archetype it generates. I will tell you where I would push back.
```
